English Writing Assignment 13

In Jules Verne’s Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea, the first twenty-three chapters are the setup, presenting the players and initiating the underwater adventure. They revolve around the mystery of a sea creature, the unmasking of a submarine, and the first underwater journey.

At the beginning, there are tales from all around the world of a powerful entity in the ocean, and many believe it to be a sea monster of epic proportions. The world’s governments are alarmed, and the Americans send a ship, the Abraham Lincoln, to investigate and hopefully destroy the beast. Professor Aronnax, a scientist, also boards the ship to study the creature. The suspense here is palpable, as no one knows what this creature might be.

The chase gets more dramatic when the ship collides with the creature, and Professor Aronnax, his servant Conseil, and the harpooner Ned Land are pitched into the ocean. Climbing onto the creature they hit, they discover it is not a creature at all, but a metal submarine, the Nautilus. The discovery of the Nautilus is a major, mind-blowing plot turn. Shortly after, they are introduced to the enigmatic Captain Nemo.

Another major part of the novel occurs when the three men and the Nautilus crew travel underwater in the submarine. Through the large windows, they see wonderful sea creatures, reefs, and other phenomena. The Nautilus, being a submarine, was also a marvel of its time, using electricity as a source of energy, a relatively new concept at the time the novel was written.

Throughout the first twenty-three chapters, the tone of the novel shifts from mystery to adventure, as the sea monster turns out to be a submarine, and the adventure begins. The sea monster mystery was a setup, and the adventure of exploring the ocean begins.
